TITTLE= 1950's American Flyer Steam Engine #320 train locomotive diecast

DESCRIPTION:


This train is a Vintage American Flyer S gauge diecast metal Steam Engine 4-6-4 Train from the 1950's. ( four wheels in the front, six wheels in the center and four wheels at the rear). Locomotive #320, MFD. by the AC GILBERT CO. The Engine still has its numbers 320 printed on the sides. This Engine has a die cast metal shell and a stamped metal frame, the wheels are die-cast metal with beutiful white walls on them. There is a locking lever for locking the train so that it allways stays at the same action (forward, stand-still or reveres). The wires from the tender to the locomotive are original and are still funtional. The AC motor has alot of back lash but is ok. All of the mechanics for the steam cyclinders to the driving wheels move freely and magesticaly. This locol even has the old shotty revese unit that some times doesn't work. :)

The coal tender, tndr, is made from diecasl metal with a stamped steel frame. The words "New York Central" are printed on the sides. Both trucks have copper pickups on the axels of the wheels, a total of four contact points.

We found the trains in a storage bin with evidence that they had been there for 45 years. We have carfully restored the trains back to working condition, (speacial thanks to yahoo's S-Train's group for all there help)

It could use a new paint job but then it wouldn't be original so I'll leave that decision up to the collector. :) besides, it looks really cool all rusty and mean!
